Our Story

Why I do what I do.

When people ask why I became a financial advisor, I don’t think about markets or investments. I think about my parents in the bleachers at my soccer games.

My dad was the first in his family to go to college — he finished dental school at 25 and built his own practice from scratch. My mom had the harder job, raising my siblings and me. They didn’t talk much about money. Instead, they showed me what it was for: being at the games, taking the trips, making being together the priority.

I started college pre-med, switched to finance, and set my sights on investment banking — then graduated in 2009, straight into the financial crisis, and that path disappeared overnight. My mom, who worked for a financial advisor, encouraged me to look at planning. The more I learned, the more I realized I’d found exactly what I wanted: a way to solve complex problems while helping people build the lives they truly want.

Seventeen years and hundreds of families later, I’ve learned that people rarely come to me with a money problem. They come with a life question that money touches.

“Wealth matters — but it’s not the goal. The goal is using it to build a meaningful life, be there for the people you love, and leave things better than you found them.”

That’s why I do what I do.
